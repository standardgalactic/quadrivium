### Complex Numbers
- **Definition**: A complex number is expressed as \( z = a + bi \), where:
  - \( a \) is the real part.
  - \( b \) is the imaginary part.
  - \( i \) is the imaginary unit, with \( i^2 = -1 \).

### Operations on Complex Numbers
- **Addition and Subtraction**: 
  - Addition: \( (a + bi) + (c + di) = (a+c) + (b+d)i \).
  - Subtraction: \( (a + bi) - (c + di) = (a-c) + (b-d)i \).

- **Multiplication**:
  - Multiply using distribution and simplify with \( i^2 = -1 \):
    \[
    (a + bi)(c + di) = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i
    \]

- **Example of Multiplication**: 
  - For \( 3 + 4i \times 3 - 3i \), the result is \( 21 + 3i \).

### Complex Conjugate
- **Definition**: The complex conjugate of \( z = a + bi \) is \( \overline{z} = a - bi \).
- **Example**:
  - For \( z = 3 + 3i \), the conjugate is \( 3 - 3i \).

### Geometric Interpretation
- On the Argand plane, a complex number \( z = a + bi \) is represented as a point or vector at coordinates \( (a, b) \).
- The conjugate reflects across the real axis.

### Important Concepts
1. **Norm of a Complex Number**:
   - Calculated by \( \|z\|^2 = a^2 + b^2 \), giving \( \|z\| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \).
   
2. **Complex Conjugate and Norm**: 
   - The product \( z\bar{z} = (a + bi)(a - bi) = a^2 + b^2 \).

3. **Argument**:
   - Represents the angle or direction of the vector from origin to point \( (a, b) \).

4. **Complex Vectors and Matrices**: 
   - Future exploration involves arranging complex numbers in vectors or matrices.

Understanding these fundamentals aids in solving equations involving complex numbers and modeling physical systems in engineering and science.
