To express \(\tan(\theta)\) in terms of \(x\), where \(x = \csc(\theta)\), we follow these steps:

1. **Relationship Understanding:**
   - Given \(x = \csc(\theta) = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{opposite}}\).
   - Thus, the hypotenuse is \(x\) and the opposite side is 1.

2. **Applying the Pythagorean Theorem:**
   - Let the adjacent side be \(p\).
   - From the theorem: \(p^2 + 1^2 = x^2\).
   - Solving for \(p\): \(p = \sqrt{x^2 - 1}\).

3. **Finding \(\tan(\theta)\):**
   - \(\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2 - 1}}\).

4. **Alternative Expression:**
   - Since \(\theta = \csc^{-1}(x)\), it follows that:
   - \(\tan(\theta) = \tan(\csc^{-1}(x)) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2 - 1}}\).

This approach uses a right triangle to express \(\tan(\theta)\) in terms of \(x\).
