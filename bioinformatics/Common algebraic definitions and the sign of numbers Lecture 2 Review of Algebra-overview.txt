This lecture introduces fundamental concepts in algebra necessary for understanding expressions, equations, terms, and their components.

1. **Constants and Variables**: 
   - Constants are fixed values that can be pinpointed on the real number line (e.g., numbers like 3, 7).
   - Variables are placeholders that can represent any constant value within an expression or equation.

2. **Expressions**:
   - An expression is a mathematical combination of constants and variables.
   - It may involve operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and the use of parentheses.
   - Example: \( \frac{5}{9} \times (\text{degrees Fahrenheit} - 32) \), which converts Fahrenheit to Celsius.

3. **Terms**:
   - A term is a single part or a combination of parts within an expression separated by addition or subtraction.
   - In the context of expressions, multiplication and division within a segment do not break it into separate terms.
   - Example: In \( 3x - 7 + 2 \), each segment divided by plus or minus signs is considered a term.

4. **Equations**:
   - An equation consists of two expressions set equal to each other, typically involving an equal sign.
   - It can be broken down into a left-hand side (LHS) and right-hand side (RHS).
   - Example: \( 3x = 9 \).
