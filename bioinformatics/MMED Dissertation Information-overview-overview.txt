This tutorial guide is designed for medical registrars preparing their master's degree dissertation as part of their final exams in the College of Medicine. It acknowledges that this requirement adds to their workload but provides strategies to handle it effectively.

Key points include:

1. **Purpose and Importance**: The dissertation enhances skills in interpreting medical research, crucial even for those not pursuing academia.

2. **Logistics and Initial Steps**:
   - Regularly check official university emails.
   - Keep personal details updated on university systems.
   - Complete necessary forms at the postgraduate office.

3. **Project Initiation**:
   - Develop a research idea independently or consult supervisor lists, like those from Professor Khan.
   - Agree with a qualified supervisor and complete a memorandum of understanding within six months of registration.

4. **Proposal Development**:
   - Work with the supervisor to draft a study proposal.
   - Submit it for review by the Department Research Committee (headed by Professor Maul) and obtain Ethics Committee approval.

5. **Data Collection and Analysis**:
   - Allocate time for data collection and analysis, using tools like Microsoft Excel.
   - Seek help if necessary and utilize available support systems.

6. **Submission Process**:
   - Submit a completed dissertation form to the postgraduate office based on surname order.
   - Ensure all documents are signed by the supervisor.

7. **Support Resources**:
   - Leverage guidance and funding from supervisors, student support committees, and the postgraduate students association.
   - Contact Audrey Winkler at the postgraduate office for further assistance.

The guide stresses proactive communication and organization to manage the dissertation process efficiently without it becoming overwhelming.
