The lecture covers seven fundamental "limit laws" that simplify calculating limits when they exist. These laws include rules for addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and handling powers and constants within functions as a variable approaches a specific value.

1. **Addition/Subtraction**: The limit of the sum or difference of two functions is the sum or difference of their limits.
   \[
   \lim_{t \to c} [f(t) + g(t)] = \lim_{t \to c} f(t) + \lim_{t \to c} g(t)
   \]

2. **Constant Multiplication**: The limit of a constant times a function is the constant times the limit of the function.
   \[
   \lim_{t \to c} [k \cdot f(t)] = k \cdot \lim_{t \to c} f(t)
   \]

3. **Product Rule**: The limit of the product of two functions equals the product of their limits.
   \[
   \lim_{t \to c} [f(t) \cdot g(t)] = \lim_{t \to c} f(t) \cdot \lim_{t \to c} g(t)
   \]

4. **Quotient Rule**: The limit of the quotient of two functions is the quotient of their limits, provided the denominator's limit is not zero.
   \[
   \lim_{t \to c} \left[\frac{f(t)}{g(t)}\right] = \frac{\lim_{t \to c} f(t)}{\lim_{t \to c} g(t)}, \quad \text{if } \lim_{t \to c} g(t) \neq 0
   \]

5. **Power Rule**: The limit of a function raised to a power is the limit of the function raised to that power.
   \[
   \lim_{t \to c} [f(t)]^n = [\lim_{t \to c} f(t)]^n
   \]

6. **Constant Function**: The limit of a constant as \( t \) approaches any value is the constant itself.
   \[
   \lim_{t \to c} k = k
   \]

7. **Identity Rule**: The limit of \( t \) as it approaches \( c \) is \( c \).
   \[
   \lim_{t \to c} t = c
   \]

In multiplication and division cases, you compute the limits separately before combining them according to the product or quotient rule.
