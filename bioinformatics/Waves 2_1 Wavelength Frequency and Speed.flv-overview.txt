The text provides an in-depth explanation of wave functions, focusing on transverse waves represented as sine functions. Key components discussed include:

1. **Wave Properties**: 
   - Amplitude: The maximum displacement from equilibrium, denoted by \( y_m \).
   - Wavelength (\( \lambda \)): The distance over which the wave's shape repeats.

2. **Mathematical Representation**:
   - Wave function: Described as a function of displacement in terms of position (\( x \)) and time (\( t \)), expressed as \( y(x, t) = y_m \sin(kx - \omega t) \).
   - Components of the wave function include amplitude (\( y_m \)), angular wave number (\( k \)), angular frequency (\( \omega \)), position (\( x \)), and time (\( t \)).

3. **Angular Wave Number**:
   - Defined as \( k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \), with units of radians per meter.
   - Represents the number of wave cycles per unit distance.

4. **Phase**:
   - The phase of a wave is expressed as \( kx - \omega t \).
   - Explained through setting specific values for \( x \) or \( t \) to simplify and analyze the function.

5. **Periodicity**:
   - Sine functions repeat every \( 2\pi \) radians.
   - This periodic nature is used to explain how waves return to similar displacements over a wavelength or time period.
