The text discusses fundamental properties of matrix arithmetic, focusing on addition and multiplication with or without scalar involvement. It assumes matrices have compatible sizes for operations like addition (requiring the same dimensions) and multiplication.

1. **Commutative Law of Addition**: Matrix addition is commutative; \( A + B = B + A \).

2. **Associative Laws**:
   - *Addition*: Adding three or more matrices doesn't depend on grouping, i.e., \((A + B) + C = A + (B + C)\).
   - *Multiplication*: The associative property holds for multiplication as well, but order must be maintained, e.g., \((AB)C = A(BC)\).

3. **Distributive Laws**:
   - Both left and right distributive properties apply: \(A(B + C) = AB + AC\) and \((A + B)C = AC + BC\).
   - It's crucial to maintain the order, especially in right distributive laws.

4. **Scalar Properties**: When scalars are involved with matrices, the scalar multiplication distributes over addition without changing matrix order: \(c(A + B) = cA + cB\) and \((c + d)A = cA + dA\).

The text emphasizes maintaining correct order in operations to avoid errors.

   - Unlike addition, matrix multiplication is not commutative; the order matters.
   - The associative law of multiplication states that the way in which matrices are grouped during multiplication does not change the product, but their order must remain unchanged.
   
3. **Distributive Laws**:
   - Both left and right distributive laws apply to matrices similarly to algebraic expressions. However, it's crucial to maintain the correct order of matrices involved.

4. **Scalar Properties**:
   - When scalars are distributed over matrix multiplication or addition, the sequence of the matrices should remain unchanged.
