To solve this problem and find the maximum velocity (\(v\)) of electrons ejected from a selenium surface, you'll follow these steps:

### Given:
- **Wavelength of incident light (\(\lambda\)):** 95 nm
- **Work function of selenium (\(W_0\)):** 5.9 eV
- **Planck's constant (\(h\)):** \(6.63 \times 10^{-34}\) J·s
- **Speed of light (\(c\)):** \(3 \times 10^8\) m/s
- **1 eV in joules:** \(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\) J
- **Mass of an electron (\(m\)):** \(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\) kg

### Steps:

1. **Convert Work Function to Joules:**
   \[
   W_0 = 5.9 \, \text{eV} \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \frac{\text{J}}{\text{eV}} = 9.44 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}
   \]

2. **Calculate the Energy of the Incident Photon (\(E\)):**
   The energy of a photon is given by:
   \[
   E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}
   \]
   Convert wavelength from nm to meters: \(95 \, \text{nm} = 95 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{m}\).

   Substitute the values:
   \[
   E = \frac{(6.63 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J·s})(3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s})}{95 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{m}}
   \]
   \[
   E = 2.09 \times 10^{-18} \, \text{J}
   \]

3. **Calculate the Maximum Kinetic Energy (\(K_{\text{max}}\)) of the Ejected Electrons:**
   Use the photoelectric equation:
   \[
   K_{\text{max}} = E - W_0
   \]
   Substitute the values:
   \[
   K_{\text{max}} = 2.09 \times 10^{-18} \, \text{J} - 9.44 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{J}
   \]
   \[
   K_{\text{max}} = 1.15 \times 10^{-18} \, \text{J}
   \]

4. **Calculate the Maximum Velocity (\(v\)) of the Ejected Electrons:**
   Use the kinetic energy formula:
   \[
   K_{\text{max}} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
   \]
   Solve for \(v\):
   \[
   v = \sqrt{\frac{2K_{\text{max}}}{m}}
   \]
   Substitute the values:
   \[
   v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 1.15 \times 10^{-18} \, \text{J}}{9.11 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg}}}
   \]
   \[
   v = \sqrt{2.52 \times 10^{12}}
   \]
   \[
   v \approx 1.59 \times 10^6 \, \text{m/s}
   \]

Thus, the maximum velocity of the ejected electrons is approximately \(1.59 \times 10^6\) m/s.
