1. **Vector Arithmetic**:
   - Vectors exhibit commutative (\( \mathbf{U} + \mathbf{V} = \mathbf{V} + \mathbf{U} \)) and associative properties.
   - The zero vector acts as the identity element, leaving any vector unchanged when added.
   - Each vector has an inverse (negative), which sums with the original to produce the zero vector.

2. **Distributive Property**:
   - Scalars can be distributed over vector addition: \( K(\mathbf{U} + \mathbf{V}) = K\mathbf{U} + K\mathbf{V} \).

3. **Norm (Length) of a Vector**:
   - In 2D, the norm is calculated as \( \|\mathbf{V}\| = \sqrt{V_1^2 + V_2^2} \).
   - For vectors not starting at the origin, subtract tail coordinates from head coordinates.
   - In 3D, extend to three components: \( \|\mathbf{V}\| = \sqrt{V_1^2 + V_2^2 + V_3^2} \).

4. **Effect of Scalar Multiplication on Norm**:
   - The norm of a scalar multiple is the absolute value of the scalar times the vector's norm: \( \|K\mathbf{V}\| = |K|\|\mathbf{V}\| \).
   - Norms are always positive, regardless of direction.

5. **Example with Trigonometry**:
   - A 2D vector \( \mathbf{V} = (V_1, V_2) \) can be expressed using trigonometric functions: 
     - \( V_1 = \|\mathbf{V}\| \cos(\theta) \).
