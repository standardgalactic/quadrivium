1. **Sequences**:
   - A sequence is an ordered set of values (e.g., \(1, 2, 3, \ldots\)), where the order matters.
   - Typically denoted by \(a_n = n\), with \(n\) being positive integers starting from 1.

2. **Graphing**:
   - Sequences are represented as discrete points on a graph because they exist only at integer values of \(n\).
   - They differ from continuous functions and do not support operations like differentiation.

3. **Limit and Convergence**:
   - A crucial aspect is understanding the sequence's behavior as \(n\) approaches infinity.
   - Sequences can converge to a specific limit \(L\) or diverge if they grow indefinitely.

4. **Examples**:
   - The sequence \(a_n = \frac{1}{n^2}\) converges to zero as \(n\) increases.
   - Conversely, the sequence \(a_n = n\) diverges because it grows without bound.
