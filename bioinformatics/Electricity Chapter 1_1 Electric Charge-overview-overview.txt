The law is mathematically expressed as being proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them, classifying it as an inverse square law. Charged particles follow the superposition principle, allowing for the addition of individual forces to find a net force, and adhere partially to the shell theorem, which simplifies calculations by treating spherical shells as point charges at their centers. The electrostatic constant (\(k\)) is related to the permittivity of free space (\(\varepsilon_0\)), with \( k = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \).
