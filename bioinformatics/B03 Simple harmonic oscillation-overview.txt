2. **Observation**: Changing the mass changes the stretching of the spring, demonstrating a direct linear relationship between force and displacement, as predicted by Hooke's Law: \( F = kX \), where \( F \) is force, \( X \) is displacement, and \( k \) is the spring constant.

3. **Force Representation**: Force can also be represented in terms of mass and acceleration (\( F = ma \)), leading to a differential equation: 
   \[
   m\frac{d^2X}{dt^2} + kX = 0
   \]
   This is derived by considering the restorative force exerted by the spring.

4. **Differential Equation**: The resulting second-order homogeneous differential equation is:
   \[
   X'' + \frac{k}{m}X = 0
   \]
   where \( X'' \) denotes the second derivative of displacement with respect to time, indicating acceleration.

5. **Solution Approach**: Solving this requires knowledge of linear algebra and differential equations. The equation can be simplified using \( \omega^2 = \frac{k}{m} \), where \( \omega \) is angular velocity, related to frequency by \( \omega = 2\pi f \).

6. **Analogy**: The motion of the mass on a spring is analogous to the circular motion of a point mass on a rotating wheel viewed edge-on, illustrating harmonic motion.
