To calculate the net force on two wires in a magnetic field:

1. **Given Information:**
   - Length of each wire (\(L\)) = 2 meters
   - Angle with x-axis (\(\theta\)) = 60 degrees
   - Current through each wire (\(I\)) = 2 amperes
   - Magnetic field (\(\mathbf{B}\)) = \(4 \, \hat{k}\) (z-direction)

2. **Length Vector Components:**
   - For wire 1:
     - \(L_{1x} = 1\) meter, \(L_{1y} = \sqrt{3}\) meters
     - Vector: \(\mathbf{L}_1 = 1 \, \hat{i} + \sqrt{3} \, \hat{j}\)
   - For wire 2 (opposite x-direction):
     - \(L_{2x} = -1\) meter, \(L_{2y} = \sqrt{3}\) meters
     - Vector: \(\mathbf{L}_2 = -1 \, \hat{i} + \sqrt{3} \, \hat{j}\)

3. **Calculate Force Using \(\mathbf{F} = I (\mathbf{L} \times \mathbf{B})\):**
   - For wire 1:
     \[
     \mathbf{F}_1 = 2 \left( (1 \, \hat{i} + \sqrt{3} \, \hat{j}) \times (4 \, \hat{k}) \right) = 8\sqrt{3} \, \hat{i} - 8 \, \hat{j}
     \]
   - For wire 2:
     \[
     \mathbf{F}_2 = 2 \left( (-1 \, \hat{i} + \sqrt{3} \, \hat{j}) \times (4 \, \hat{k}) \right) = 8\sqrt{3} \, \hat{i} + 8 \, \hat{j}
     \]

4. **Net Force Calculation:**
   - Combine forces:
     \[
     \mathbf{F}_{\text{net}} = (8\sqrt{3} \, \hat{i} - 8 \, \hat{j}) + (8\sqrt{3} \, \hat{i} + 8 \, \hat{j})
     \]
   - Simplify:
     - \(i\) component: \(16\sqrt{3}\)
     - \(j\) component: \(0\)

The net force on the wires is \(16\sqrt{3} \, \hat{i}\) Newtons.
