This short episode of "Let's Talk Surgery" acknowledges the life and contributions of Chris Barnard, who performed the first heart transplant at Groote Schuur Hospital in Cape Town. It highlights the significant impact of this milestone in medicine. The discussion then shifts to Alan Cormack, whose work on the mathematical foundations of the CT scanner earned him a Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1979 alongside Godfrey Hounsfield. Born in Johannesburg and educated at the University of Cape Town, Cormack's work has had a profound influence on modern medical imaging. The episode encourages viewers to explore more about Cormack through available resources and links.
