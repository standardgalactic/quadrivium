The text discusses the Doppler effect and how it applies to sound waves, particularly in scenarios involving relative motion between a source and an observer. The Doppler effect describes the change in observed frequency of a wave when there is relative motion between the source and the observer.

### Key Points:

1. **Doppler Effect Equation**: 
   - The equation for the observed frequency considers:
     - Frequency of the source
     - Velocity of the observer (positive or negative depending on direction)
     - Velocity of the source (also positive or negative based on direction)

2. **Mechanical Waves**:
   - The discussion focuses on mechanical waves, which require a medium to travel through.
   - Sound is an example where the Doppler effect can be observed.

3. **Directionality**:
   - A unit vector from observer to source helps determine sign (positive or negative) for velocities.
   - If both move towards each other, velocity signs are aligned such that they increase the frequency.
