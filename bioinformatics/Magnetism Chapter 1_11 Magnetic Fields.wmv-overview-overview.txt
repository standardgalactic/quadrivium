The text provides a step-by-step solution to a physics problem involving magnetic dipole moments and torques for a current-carrying loop. Here's a summary:

### Given:
- Number of Turns (\(n\)): 1
- Radius of the Loop (\(r\)): 15 cm or 0.15 m
- Current (\(i\)): 2.6 A
- Angle with Magnetic Field (\(\theta\)): 41 degrees
- Magnetic Field (\(B\)): Not specified

### Calculations:

#### 1. Magnetic Dipole Moment (\(\mu\)):
The magnetic dipole moment is calculated using:
\[
\mu = n \cdot i \cdot A
\]
where \(A\) (the area of the loop) for a circle is:
\[
A = \pi r^2 = \pi (0.15)^2 = 0.0706858 \, \text{m}^2
\]

Thus:
\[
\mu = 1 \cdot 2.6 \cdot 0.0706858 = 0.18378108 \, \text{A}\cdot\text{m}^2
\]
Rounded to four decimal places: \(0.1838 \, \text{A}\cdot\text{m}^2\).

#### 2. Torque (\(\tau\)):
The torque on the loop is given by:
\[
\tau = \mu B \sin(\theta)
\]

Substituting known values:
\[
\tau = 0.18378108 \cdot B \cdot \sin(41^\circ)
\]
\(\sin(41^\circ) \approx 0.6561\), so:
\[
\tau \approx 0.1205 \cdot B \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}
\]

### Conclusion:
- **Magnetic Dipole Moment (\(\mu\)):** \(0.1838 \, \text{A}\cdot\text{m}^2\)
- **Torque (\(\tau\)):** \(0.1205 \cdot B \, \text{N}\cdot\text{m}\) (depends on the magnetic field strength \(B\))

To determine the exact torque, the value of \(B\) must be known.
