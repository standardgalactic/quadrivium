1. **Linear Momentum**:
   - Defined as mass times velocity (\( \mathbf{p} = m\mathbf{v} \)).

2. **Angular Momentum**:
   - Defined as the cross product of the position vector (\(\mathbf{r}\)) and linear momentum vector (\(\mathbf{p}\)).
   - Formula: \(\mathbf{L} = \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{p}\).
   - In a two-dimensional system, this results in a vector perpendicular to the plane of motion.

3. **Cross Product**:
   - For 2D vectors (\(x_1, y_1\) and \(x_2, y_2\)), the cross product is calculated as \(x_1y_2 - x_2y_1\).
   - For 3D vectors, it involves a determinant with unit vectors \(\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{j}, \mathbf{k}\) on top.

4. **Torque**:
   - Defined as the rate of change of angular momentum.
   - Related to force by \(\boldsymbol{\tau} = \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F}\), where \(\mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a}\) (mass times acceleration).
   - The maximum torque occurs when the angle between \(\mathbf{r}\) and \(\mathbf{F}\) is 90 degrees.
