The expression for acceleration becomes:
\[ \mathbf{a} = \frac{d^2s}{dt^2} \mathbf{T} + \kappa \left(\frac{ds}{dt}\right)^2 \mathbf{N} \]

This decomposition of acceleration into tangential and normal components is useful for understanding motion along curves and plays a role in deriving equations related to curvature.
