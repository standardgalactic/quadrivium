The formula given for the actual potential difference across a battery is: 

\[ \text{Actual Voltage} = \text{EMF} - I \times r \]

where \( I \) is the current and \( r \) is the internal resistance of the battery.

Power in the circuit, represented by \( IV \), can be expressed as:

\[ P = I(\text{EMF} - Ir) \]
\[ P = I\text{EMF} - I^2r \]

Here, \( I\text{EMF} \) is the total power provided by the battery, used for both moving charge carriers against their potential difference and overcoming internal resistance. The term \( I^2r \) represents the power lost as heat due to internal resistance.

In summary, while the EMF indicates the maximum potential energy available from a battery, the actual voltage delivered is reduced by the internal resistance when current flows, leading to some of the power being dissipated as heat internally within the battery.
