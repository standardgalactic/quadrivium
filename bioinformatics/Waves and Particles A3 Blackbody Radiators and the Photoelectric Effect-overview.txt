   - If different frequencies are used, electrons will have varying kinetic energies.
   - For light bulbs emitting red, green, and violet light: Violet (higher frequency) yields photoelectrons with higher kinetic energy than red (lower frequency), assuming all exceed the metal's work function.

2. **Intensity vs. Frequency**:
   - Increasing a red light bulb's power (100W vs. 10W) doesn't change the kinetic energy of ejected electrons if both have the same frequency.
   - To increase photoelectrons' maximum kinetic energy, increase the incident light frequency.
   - Intensity affects the number of ejected electrons and current but not their kinetic energy or momentum.

3. **Photon vs. Particle Energy/Momentum**:
   - For particles: Total energy is \( \text{KE} + \text{PE} \) (non-relativistic), momentum is mass times velocity.
   - For photons: Energy is \( hf \); momentum is \( h/\lambda \).
