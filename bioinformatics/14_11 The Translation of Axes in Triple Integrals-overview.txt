2. **Recalculate the Original Function:** Express the function \( f(x, y, z) \) in terms of the new coordinates \( u, v, w \), essentially substituting \( \mathbf{x} \) with \( \mathbf{t}(u, v, w) \).

3. **Calculate the Jacobian Determinant:** Determine the absolute value of the determinant of the Jacobian matrix for the transformation from \( (x, y, z) \) to \( (u, v, w) \).

4. **Integrate Over New Axes:** Integrate over the new variables \( du, dv, dw \), with the region defined in these new coordinates.
