When the circuit is closed, electrons move between the plates due to the electric field from the battery: one plate becomes positively charged, and the other negatively charged. Once equilibrium is reached, there's no current flow, as the potential across the plates equals that of the battery. The capacitance (\(C\)) of a capacitor depends solely on its geometry (plate area, separation distance) and not on the charge (\(Q\)) or voltage (\(V\)). It’s defined by \(C = Q/V\), where the unit is the farad.

This text provides an overview of capacitance, focusing on capacitors and their properties. Here's a summary:

1. **Basic Concept**: A capacitor consists of two conductive plates separated by some distance, forming the core component in circuits for storing charge.

2. **Charging Process**:
   - When connected to a battery via a switch, electrons move due to an electric field created by the battery.
   - Electrons are drawn towards the positive terminal from one plate and repelled to the other, charging one plate negatively and the opposite positively.
   - Once equilibrium is reached, no more current flows as the potential difference across the plates equals that of the battery.

3. **Capacitance**:
   - Defined as the proportionality constant between charge (Q) on the capacitor's plates and the electric potential difference (V) across them: \( C = \frac{Q}{V} \).
   - Measured in farads, where one farad equals one coulomb per volt.
   - Capacitance depends solely on the geometry of the capacitor and not on the charge or potential.

4. **Calculations**:
   - Example calculations show how capacitance remains constant irrespective of changes in voltage or charge.
   - If a capacitor with a known capacitance is subjected to different potentials, the charge can be recalculated using \( Q = CV \).

5. **Dependence on Geometry**:
   - For parallel plate capacitors, the electric field (E) between plates is constant and perpendicular to them.
   - Using Gauss's law, the total charge (Q) is related to the permittivity of free space (\( \varepsilon_0 \)), the electric field (E), and surface area (A).
   - The potential difference (\( V \)) across the plates is calculated as \( V = Ed \), where d is the distance between plates.

6. **Conclusion**:
   - Capacitance is a geometric property, determined by factors such as plate area and separation.
   - Changing either charge or voltage results in changes to the other, while maintaining constant capacitance.
