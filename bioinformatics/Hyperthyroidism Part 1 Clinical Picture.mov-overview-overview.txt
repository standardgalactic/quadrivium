While primarily focused on specific diseases, it also highlights the importance of understanding thyroid function. Hyperthyroidism is often linked to primary overproduction of hormones, whereas "thyrotoxicosis" can result from excess hormones from various sources.

Causes of thyrotoxicosis include Graves' disease (most common), toxic multinodular goiter, solitary toxic adenoma, and Hashimoto's thyroiditis in its thyrotoxic phase. Other causes are drug-induced thyrotoxicosis (e.g., amiodarone), hormone intake, TSH-secreting pituitary tumors, and certain cancers.

Symptoms of hyperthyroidism are diverse due to the hypermetabolic state caused by excess hormones. These include weight loss, fatigue from muscle weakness, neuropsychiatric issues like restlessness and anxiety, decreased concentration and memory, increased bowel movements, hormonal changes affecting menstrual cycles in women and libido in men, peripheral vasodilation leading to tachycardia, and potential cardiac complications.
