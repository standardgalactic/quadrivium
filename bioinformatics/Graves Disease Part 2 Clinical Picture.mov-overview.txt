1. **Symptoms and Signs**:
   - Patients often present with an enlarged thyroid gland (goiter) that may be highly vascular, making a bruit audible.
   - Symptoms include sympathetic overactivity like tachycardia, arrhythmias, tremors, anxiety, staring gaze, and lid lag due to hypermetabolism.
   - Common issues involve eye problems resulting from inflammation and infiltration of the retroorbital space by immune cells. These can cause protrusion of the eyes, leading to corneal changes.
   - Eye symptoms range from mild (most patients) to severe in 3-5% of cases, which may require glucocorticoid therapy or surgery.
   - Around 10% have eye issues without hyperthyroidism but with circulating TSH receptor antibodies.
   - A small percentage (less than 5%) develop pretibial myxedema, characterized by thickened, discolored skin and edema over the shins. Severe cases may require treatment with topical steroids or compressive dressings.

2. **Laboratory Findings**:
   - Increased levels of thyroid hormones T3 and T4, along with suppressed TSH.
   - Scintigraphy shows diffuse increased uptake of tracer elements in the thyroid.

1. **Symptoms and Signs:**
   - An enlarged thyroid that might be visibly or audibly vascularity.
   - General sympathetic overactivity, including tachycardia, arrhythmias, tremors, anxiety, wide staring gaze, and lid lag.
   - Weight loss due to a hypermetabolic state.
   - Ophthalmopathy is common, with most cases being mild. It involves infiltration of the retroorbital space by immune cells, leading to eye protrusion and possible corneal changes. Severe cases can result in pain, double vision, or vision loss, requiring interventions like glucocorticoid therapy or surgery.

   - Skin manifestations occur in a small percentage and include scaly thickening over the anterior tibial region and hair loss. More severe forms can involve purple-brown skin lesions, non-pitting edema, or even elephantiasis-like changes.
   - Rare complications can include clubbing of fingers/toes with local edema and periosteal bone formation.
