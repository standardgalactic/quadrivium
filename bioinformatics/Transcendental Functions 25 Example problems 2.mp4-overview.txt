The text provides several examples of solving equations for \( x \) using logarithms:

1. **Example 1**: Solve \( 2^x = \ln(4) \).
   - Take the natural log: \( x \cdot \ln(2) = \ln(\ln(4)) \).
   - Solve for \( x \): \( x = \frac{\ln(\ln(4))}{\ln(2)} \).

2. **Example 2**: Solve \( 100e^{10x} = 200 \).
   - Divide by 100: \( e^{10x} = 2 \).
   - Take the natural log: \( 10x = \ln(2) \).
   - Solve for \( x \): \( x = \frac{\ln(2)}{10} \).

3. **Example 3**: Solve \( e^{\frac{x}{1000}} = a \).
   - Take the natural log: \( \frac{x}{1000} = \ln(a) \).
   - Solve for \( x \): \( x = 1000 \cdot \ln(a) \).

4. **Example 4**: Solve \( e^{-0.3x} = 3^3 \).
   - Take the natural log: \( -0.3x = \ln(27) \).
   - Use properties of logs: \( x = -10 \cdot \ln(3) \).

5. **Example 5**: Solve \( e^{kx} = \frac{1}{2} \).
   - Take the natural log: \( kx = \ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) \).
   - Simplify: \( x = -\frac{\ln(2)}{k} \).

6. **Example 6**: Solve \( 0.2^x = 0.4 \).
   - Take the natural log: \( x \cdot \ln(0.2) = \ln(0.4) \).
   - Solve for \( x \): \( x = \frac{\ln(0.4)}{\ln(0.2)} \).

7. **Example 7**: Solve \( \sqrt{x} = (\ln(a))^2 \).
   - Square both sides: \( x = [(\ln(a))^2]^2 \).
   - Simplify: \( x = 4(\ln(a))^2 \). 

Each example demonstrates using logarithmic properties to isolate and solve for \( x \).
