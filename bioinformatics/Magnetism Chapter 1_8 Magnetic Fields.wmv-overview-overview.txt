Here's a summary of the text on cyclotron operation:

1. **Structure and Functionality**: A cyclotron is composed of two half-circle copper containers with open sides where particles enter. These structures guide charged particles along a semicircular path.

2. **Magnetic and Electric Fields**: The cyclotron employs a strong magnetic field to direct particles in circular motion, while an oscillating electric field across the gaps accelerates them each time they cross from one half-container to the other.

3. **Particle Acceleration**: Particles like protons are injected at the center and gain energy through alternating electric fields as they move between the halves. The magnetic field maintains their circular path, with the oscillator frequency matching the particles' cyclotron frequency for continuous acceleration.

4. **Key Formula**: The necessary magnetic field strength \( B \) is calculated using:
   \[
   B = \frac{2\pi m f}{q}
   \]
   where \( m \) is mass, \( f \) is the oscillator frequency, and \( q \) is charge.

5. **Kinetic Energy Calculation**: The kinetic energy at maximum radius (edge of the cyclotron) is:
   \[
   KE = 2\pi^2 m R^2 f^2
   \]
   where \( R \) is the maximum radius.

6. **Example Problem**: For a deuteron with mass \( 3.34 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg} \), charge like a proton, in a cyclotron with frequency \( 12 \, \text{MHz} \) and radius \( 53 \, \text{cm} \):
   - Calculate the magnetic field.
   - Determine the final kinetic energy at the edge.

7. **Proton Synchrotron Solution**: To overcome limitations like large radii or relativistic effects at high velocities, a proton synchrotron adjusts the oscillation frequency with particle velocity to maintain consistent acceleration and trajectory without outward spiraling.
