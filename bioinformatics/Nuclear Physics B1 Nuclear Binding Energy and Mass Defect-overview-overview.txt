1. **Nuclear Binding Energy**: This is the energy required to keep nucleons (protons and neutrons) together within an atomic nucleus. A larger binding energy indicates a more stable nucleus.

2. **Mass Defect**: When nucleons combine to form a nucleus, their total mass decreases compared to when they are separate. The difference in mass is called the mass defect, which transforms into nuclear binding energy according to Einstein's equation \(E = mc^2\).

3. **Atomic Mass Units (amu)**: These units simplify calculations in physics and chemistry. One amu is one-twelfth of a carbon-12 atom’s mass. Energy conversions use the factor 931.5 MeV per amu.

4. **Stability and Binding Energy**: Nuclei with higher binding energy per nucleon are more stable. The text discusses comparing nuclei like helium isotopes (e.g., Helium-3) to illustrate these principles.
