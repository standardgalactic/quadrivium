A commutative operation remains unchanged when the order of its elements is swapped, exemplified by symmetry along a diagonal in ordered pairs. Associativity means that grouping does not affect the result, as shown by equivalent outcomes whether (A and B) is grouped with C or A is grouped with (B and C).
