C + i S method

series called Auxiliary series such that when it is combined with the

forms :

Series in geometrical progression
(ii) 
Series in arithmetic-geometric progression
(iii) 
Binomial series
(iv) 
Exponential series
(v) 
Logarithmic series
(vi) 
Gregory’s series
We denote the cosine series by C and sine series by 5, where the 
angles are in A.P. Then we find the sum C + i S which can easily be 
separated into real and imaginary parts by the method already learnt.
